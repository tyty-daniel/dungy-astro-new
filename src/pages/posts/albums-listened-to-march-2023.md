A start of a new series I want to try where I try (and fail) to review albums I have listened to within a month. I aim to do this every month, though we shall see how long that lasts...

I actually listened to a lot more albums in March, though I didn't try to record my feelings on them. I'll aim to do that for April's blog post!

## Spyro Gyra - Catching the Sun (1980)

Rating: 6/10

Bought this at my city’s record fair. A Japanese pressing with an OBI. I loved the cover of it so thought why not! 
A jazz-funk/fusion album from the Jazz group Spyro Gyra, a band popular in the jazz scene in the 70s and 80s, Catching the Sun is an enjoyable listen that’s pleasant for a summer listen. The record has bright funky tracks, then tones it down in other tracks, going for a nice smooth jazz vibe. Although not up there for me, its still a decent listen and I’ll probably put this on as background music on a hot night.

Highlight tracks:
- Autumn of Our Love
- Percolator
- Safari

## Yussef Kamaal - Black Focus (2016)

Rating: 8/10

Also bought this at my city’s record fair! A collaboration between Kamaal Williams and Yussef Dayes, this jazz-funk album is electrifying and fresh. The drums on the album are energetic, the keyboard/synthesizer brings a nice electronic feel to the album that a spacey and cosmic vibe breath through. I’ve relistened to a lot of songs on the album and is certainly a record I will revisit many times.#

Highlight tracks:
- Lowrider
- Strings of Light
- Joint 17


## Modest by default - THE CURSE OF COLONIALISM II (2022)

Rating: 7/10

Vaporwave’s offshoot genre ‘Barber Beats’ has had its many albums in my YouTube recommended page for months now. I’m a big fan of Haircuts for Men, so I wasn’t really opposed to it, thought there were tons to choose from. 

Modest by default is a prolific artist, with around 20 albums produced under 2 years. Their album collection THE CURSE OF COLONIALISM all feature a woman soldier from the Peshmerga forces, and serve as the backdrop to the juxtaposing mall background music. Perhaps a commentary within itself.

Regardless, the music itself is pleasant and calming, with peak luxe Miami Vice vibes that you can zone out to or have on in the background. 

Highlight tracks:
- Temperature drop
- Dirty hands
- What a beautiful morning


## Cicada - 栖居在溪源之上

Rating: 8/10

Serene neoclassical album by Taiwanese project Cicada. The soothing journey through this album immerses you in a lush spring forest with beautiful strings, helping you find yourself by a calming waterfall.

For lack of a better term, it “feels like you’re in a Ghibli film”. Some of the tracks also feel like the background music of a village RPG… Not sure if that’s the intention from the artists but I enjoy it nonetheless. 

Highlight tracks:
- 巨木曾在的痕跡 Remains of Ancient Trees
- 松​叶​上​的​鸟​与​水​珠 Birds and Moist Pine Needles
- 穿​过​雾​雨​森​林 Foggy Rain
